CentSense

Opt-in & consent documentation

CentSense is a personal budgeting tool built and operated by a single individual, for that same individual's own use. There is no public sign-up, no customer base, and no third party ever receives a message from this system.

Who opts in

The account owner (developer) links their own bank and card accounts via Plaid, and registers their own phone number as the sole recipient of alerts. This is a single, direct, self-initiated action — the same person builds the tool, provides the phone number, and receives every message.

What the recipient agrees to

By providing their own number to the app's configuration, the account owner consents to:

  • Receiving a text message after each purchase on their linked accounts, showing budget usage for that spending category.
  • Receiving one end-of-day summary text of that day's transactions.
  • Message frequency of roughly a few texts per day, tied directly to their own real purchases.
  • Standard message and data rates may apply, per their own mobile carrier plan.

Sample opt-in confirmation message

Sent once, when linking a phone number

"Welcome to CentSense. You'll get a text after each purchase and a daily summary, based on your own budgets. Reply STOP at any time to stop receiving messages."

Opt-out

Since the sender and recipient are the same individual, opting out is as simple as the account owner disabling their own notifications or removing their number from the app's configuration. Standard STOP/HELP keyword handling is supported for compliance.
